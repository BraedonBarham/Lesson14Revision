Oil Requires Increasing Investment to Support the U.S. Consumer Base

By: Braedon Barham

Published: 4/15/23

WHY?

Global oil demand is expected to increase, and U.S. production has followed similarly. In order to support future oil demand, the U.S. must keep increasing oil production, otherwise the nation's consumers will be paying higher prices for harder to access oil.

Shale is easy and cheap to access oil, but top performing shale fields are declining in quantity. Because shale wells fall in production quickly, new wells must continuously be drilled. Because fewer quality shale fields are avaible, a larger quantity of wells will have to be drilled on poorer quality land, driving up required investment costs just to keep total U.S. proudction levels flat.

DUCs - Drilled but Uncomplete Wells, are oil wells that have been drilled, but don't yet produce any oil because they haven't been completed. They're very important for oil producers and the U.S. because they serve as a quick way to create oil production and thus smooth out oil market supply shocks. DUC supply being low is a real problem because it introduces the possibility of price stability which is a national concern. In order to support the U.S. consumer base reliably, the number of DUCs need to increase, requiring increased investments from recent years to make up for the shortfall in supply.
